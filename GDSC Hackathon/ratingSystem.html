Rating System

We conduct a comprehensive analysis of the materials comprising each product. Considering manufacturing processes, practices, and end-of-life disposal is crucial when assessing sustainability. However, understanding the origins of materials is also a significant contributing factor to the overall importance of product sustainability.

Take viscose, for example, the third most widely used textile fiber globally. It is imperative to inquire about its ethical sourcing. Ordering a top may not be a transgression, but considering it's sustainability is a responsibility every individual should shoulder.

Shopping is simple. Our objective is to simplify and make transparent the identification process for users. To evaluate the sustainability of a product, it is essential to scrutinize the entire supply chain, encompassing raw material extraction, production, and disposal.

We scrutinize renowned brands such as Patagonia, Reformation, Adidas, Levi's, etc., employing in-depth material comparisons and production analyses to provide users with valuable insights into the environmental and ethical practices of these brands.

Our initiative is dedicated to closing the information gap on environmentally sustainable materials, guiding users to recognize those with adverse environmental impacts and explore more Eco-friendly alternatives.

The rating system we've established is crafted to assess products through these material comparisons, with the brand's background playing a critical role in determining the final rating.

It's crucial to underscore that our focus extends beyond the product per se; we recognize the equal significance of how materials are sourced. Through our insights into both the materials utilized and the practices of the brands involved, we empower users to make decisions that go beyond product quality, taking into account the broader environmental and ethical considerations associated with their choices.

Our Rating Scale

0-49%

  • poor manufacturing process
  • poor material production
  • poor environmental impact

50-69%

  • satisfactory manufacturing process
  • satisfactory material production
  • satisfactory environmental impact

70-99%

  • good manufacturing process and sustainable alternatives
  • good material production
  • good environmental impact
